## About section (paste into "About")

> **The whole of history, on one spinning globe.**
>
> Chronos Earth is a free website where you drag a timeline across 250 million
> years and watch the world change — continents drift, ice ages come and go,
> monuments rise stone by stone, borders breathe, battles unfold on the real
> terrain where they were fought. Dive from orbit to Stonehenge at 2500 BCE,
> watch the Tower of London grow through five reigns, drain the oceans to see
> the land bridges our ancestors walked.
>
> **It's free and it stays free.** No ads, no accounts, no tracking, no
> paywall. The whole thing runs on open data (NASA, Wikidata, Wikipedia) and
> free hosting — a deliberate design law: the site itself costs nothing to run,
> so it can never be held to ransom by server bills.
>
> **So what does your support pay for?** The crew. Chronos Earth is built by
> one person — the Captain — steering a crew of AI shipwrights who model the
> monuments, choreograph the battles and harvest the history, night after
> night. Your pledge keeps that AI crew on retainer and the Captain at the
> helm, and it directly decides what gets built next.
>
> Every patron gets their name on the ship's manifest, and the higher tiers
> steer the telescope: vote on the next wonder, or nominate the monument, city
> or battlefield we build after that.
>
> Come travel time with us. ⚓

**£6/month — Navigator**

> - **Vote on what's next** — each month a poll picks the next monument, wonder
>   or battle the crew builds
> - Behind-the-scenes: the workshop gallery, the modeller's render sheets, the
>   before/after of every rework
> Thank-you blurb: *"Welcome to the chart table, Navigator — you steer what we build next. 🧭"*

**£12/month — Maker's Circle**
> Everything above, plus:
> - **Nominate a site** — name a monument, castle, city or battlefield and it
>   goes straight into the build queue (one nomination a month)
> - Your nomination credited on its info panel when it ships
> Thank-you blurb: *"Welcome to the Maker's Circle — tell the crew what to raise next, and we'll raise it. 🏗"*
